# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Analysts

### Role Summary
QA Analysts own quality assurance strategy, test planning, and validation of acceptance criteria. They ensure deliverables meet quality standards before release and collaborate closely with developers and product teams.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Perform functional, integration, and regression testing
- Identify and document defects with clear reproduction steps
- Collaborate with developers to resolve quality issues
- Define Definition of Done criteria from a quality perspective
- Participate in release readiness assessments

### Goals
- Ensure product quality and user satisfaction
- Reduce defects reaching production
- Enable confident, predictable releases

### Interaction Points
- **With Developers**: review acceptance criteria, validate PRs, triage test failures
- **With Product Managers**: clarify requirements and edge cases for test coverage
- **With Project Managers**: provide quality status in weekly syncs and release readiness reports
- **In Execution**: participate in sprint planning, daily standups, and pre-release verification

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and triage discussions
- Release readiness sign-offs
- Quality metrics and trend analysis

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, deployments, and production monitoring. They enable reliable, repeatable delivery and serve as a bridge between development and operations.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Automate deployment and rollback processes
- Manage infrastructure, containers, and cloud services
- Monitor system health, performance, and security
- Respond to incidents and coordinate incident response
- Document runbooks and disaster recovery procedures

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, repeatable deployments
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce deployment risk and manual overhead

### Interaction Points
- **With Developers**: support integration testing, provide deployment feedback
- **With Project Managers**: report deployment status and incident impact
- **With QA Analysts**: coordinate staging environment access and pre-release smoke tests
- **In Release Management**: execute deployments, monitor rollouts, manage rollbacks

### Typical Communication
- Deployment schedules and status updates
- Infrastructure and incident reports
- Performance and availability metrics
- Runbooks and operational documentation

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather requirements, document business processes, and translate stakeholder needs into actionable work for engineering and product teams. They help clarify priorities and ensure solutions align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirement gathering
- Document business processes and current state analysis
- Create requirement specifications and use cases
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Support backlog refinement and prioritization discussions
- Facilitate communication between business and technical teams

### Goals
- Ensure clarity of requirements and reduce rework
- Align technical solutions with business strategy
- Minimize miscommunication and scope creep

### Interaction Points
- **With Product Managers**: refine backlog items, clarify success metrics and acceptance criteria
- **With Developers**: explain business context and edge cases during implementation
- **With Stakeholders**: gather feedback and validate solution alignment
- **In Planning**: participate in kickoff and planning sessions to explain business drivers

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and use case specifications
- Stakeholder summary notes and decision logs
- Backlog refinement meeting discussions
- Business impact analysis and process documentation

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers craft user experiences and interfaces that are usable, accessible, and aligned with product goals. They collaborate with product and development teams to translate requirements into user-friendly solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Define user interaction flows and information architecture
- Ensure accessibility and design consistency
- Iterate designs based on feedback and testing
- Provide design specifications and assets to developers

### Goals
- Maximize user satisfaction and adoption
- Reduce support burden through intuitive design
- Maintain consistent brand and UX across products

### Interaction Points
- **With Product Managers**: understand user needs and product strategy
- **With Developers**: provide design specifications, iterate on implementation
- **With QA Analysts**: define acceptance criteria for user experience and usability
- **In Planning & Execution**: participate in design reviews and sprint planning

### Typical Communication
- Design mockups, prototypes, and design systems
- User research findings and usability test results
- Design specifications and handoff documentation
- Design review meeting participation
